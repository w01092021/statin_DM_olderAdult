Project title: Effectiveness and safety of statins for primary prevention of cardiovascular disease in old and very old adults with type 2 diabetes: a target trial emulation study

This project examines the benefits and risks associated with statin use for primary prevention among older adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) and elevated low‑density lipoprotein cholesterol (LDL‑C ≥ 2.6 mmol/L).

Participants were categorized into the following age groups for analysis:
(1) 60–74 years (used as a benchmark group to test the validity of the emulated target trial);
(2) 75–84 years;
(3) ≥85 years.

The outcomes of interest included:
(1)	overall incidence of CVDs (a composite outcome of myocardial infarction, heart failure, and stroke);
(2)	myocardial infarction;
(3)	heart failure;
(4)	stroke;
(5)	all-cause mortality;
(6)	muscle-related adverse events;
(7)	liver dysfunction.

The study used pooled logistic regression models to estimate hazard ratios for each outcome associated with statin use in both:
(1)	intention‑to‑treat analysis;
(2)	per‑protocol analyses.

All analyses were performed in Stata/MP, version 17.0.
